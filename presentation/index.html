01 · Problem & Validation
14,000 adults. A transit system that leaves them behind.
No Transit
Maria, North Greenbush
Enrolled in a GED program at Capital District EOC in Troy. The nearest CDTA bus stop is 3.1 miles from her home — there is no bus she can even board. A rideshare costs $22 round-trip, ~20% of her weekly income.
Unreachable Stop
James, Castleton-on-Hudson
Enrolled at Questar III BOCES in Castleton. The nearest bus stop is 5.2 miles from the facility — no way to reach it on foot. Program requires 3 days/week attendance. He dropped out after month one.
Hidden Barrier
Aisha, Schenectady
Traveling to adult ed programs in Troy. A Route 370 stop is 0.2 miles from her door — every planning tool marks her "served." But the ride to Troy takes 65 min. Her round trip is 2+ hours.

Transit deserts disproportionately cut off low-income, car-free adults from education and workforce programs. These three stories are what that systemic failure looks like on the ground. GoTo is built for CDTA planners and adult ed administrators who need data to act.

Why GoTo is unique: Google Maps shows existing routes — it doesn’t identify gaps or score interventions. CDTA’s internal tools are proprietary. GoTo is the only open, public-facing platform that identifies deserts, scores proposed fixes, and quantifies equity impact in one view.
Sources: Census ACS 5-yr · CDTA schedules · NYSED facility data
02 · Use of Open Data
Four open datasets.
One unified view.
CDTA Published Schedules
6 real routes (85, 87, 22, 370, 286, 289), 51 stops with GPS coordinates, headways, and terminus data. Used to compute walk + wait + ride transit times.
Census ACS 5-Year Estimates
Car-free household percentages and median income by census tract — used to identify equity need and weight the route scoring model.
NYSED Facility Locations
14 adult education sites across the Capital District — addresses geocoded and validated against CDTA stop proximity for desert classification.
OpenStreetMap via OSRM
Real road-following geometry for all 7 proposed routes — pre-fetched at build time so routes trace actual streets, not straight lines.

05 · Use of AI
AI isn't a label here.
It's doing real work.
Route Scoring Engine
Multi-factor scoring model computes a 0–100 score per proposed route using: population need, car-free %, destination proximity (hospitals, schools, jobs within 2mi), cost efficiency ($/mile), and network overlap penalty. Fully explainable — every point is shown to the user.
Gemini Analysis Panel IN PROGRESS
Currently rule-based text generation using real facility data. Architecture is live-API ready — wiring Google Gemini to generate genuinely contextual insights per location. Prompt: facility access data + equity metrics → 2-sentence plain-language recommendation.
Perplexity Computer
Used throughout the build: web search to find real CDTA data, Census ACS queries, Python to compute transit-time classifications, OSRM API to fetch road geometry, and code generation for the entire platform. One AI tool, end-to-end.
Next: Live Gemini + Foot Traffic
Replacing rule-based insights with live Gemini API calls. Adding pedestrian foot traffic data to direct route proposals toward corridors people are already using.
